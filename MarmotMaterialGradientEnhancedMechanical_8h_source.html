33  /*
34  Abstract basic class for Mechanical materials.
35  'Mechanical' is meant in the 'most general sense', i.e., any material which describes a mechanical (cauchy)
36  stress - deformation relationship (e.g, hyperelastic, hypoelastic, elasto-plastic, visco-elastic materials)
37 
38  σ = f (σ, dxdX, t, .. ),
39 
40  formulated incrementally as σ_np = f (σ_n, dxdX_n, dxdX_np, Δt, t_n, .. )
41 
42  Algorithmic tangent: dσdF = d σ_np d (dxdX_np)
43 
44  Format:
45 
46  / d σ_11 d F_00, d σ_11 d F_10, d σ_11 d F_20, d σ_11 d F_01, \
47  | d σ_22 d F_00, |
48  | d σ_33 d F_00, |
49  | ... |
50  | ... |
51  \ ... /
52 
53  such that it can be interpreted as a column major 6x3x3 tensor (4th order, left voigt tensor)
54  */
